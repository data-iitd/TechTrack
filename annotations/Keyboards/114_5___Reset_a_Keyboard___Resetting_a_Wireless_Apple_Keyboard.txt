0 - Resetting a Wireless Apple Keyboard

1 - Turn off your keyboard.
Turn off your keyboard. Press and hold the Power button on the keyboard for at least three seconds.

2 - Open your Mac's Apple menu .
Open your Mac's Apple menu . Click the Apple logo in the top-left corner of the screen. A drop-down menu will appear.

3 - Click System Preferences.
Click System Preferences. It's near the top of the drop-down menu.

4 - Click Bluetooth.
Click Bluetooth. This  icon is in the middle of the System Preferences window.
- If Bluetooth isn't on, click Turn Bluetooth On on the left side of the window before continuing.

5 - Press and hold your keyboard's Power button.
Press and hold your keyboard's Power button. Don't release the button when the keyboard powers on.

6 - Select the keyboard.
Select the keyboard. While still holding down the power button, click the keyboard's name in the Bluetooth menu.

7 - Release the Power button when prompted for a code.
Release the Power button when prompted for a code. Once your Mac asks you to type in a pairing code, you can release the Power button.

8 - Enter the pairing code.
Enter the pairing code. Type in your keyboard's code, then press ‚èé Return. This will cause your freshly reset keyboard to pair with your Mac.
