0 - Laptops without a NumLock Key

1 - Determine if your laptop should have a NumLock key.
Determine if your laptop should have a NumLock key. Different laptop models have different numeric pad configurations.
- If your U, I, and O keys have 4, 5, and 6 printed in the lower corner, you have an older laptop with an alternate numeric pad. See the next section for details on using it.
- The ThinkPad line of laptops do not use an alternate numeric pad. You'll need to use the method in this section as a workaround. Some larger models have a dedicated numeric pad.

2 - Open the Start menu or Start screen on your computer.
Open the Start menu or Start screen on your computer. Click the "Start" button in the lower-right corner of the desktop. In many versions of Windows, this is just a Windows icon. The Start menu will appear above the button.
- If you are using Windows 8 and don't see the Start button, press âŠž Win on the keyboard. This will open the Start screen.

3 - Type "keyboard" in the Start menu.
Type "keyboard" in the Start menu. You can start typing immediately when the Start menu or screen is open to begin searching. You'll see "On-Screen Keyboard" in the search results. If your Lenovo laptop does not have a NumLock key, you can use the Windows On-Screen Keyboard to access the numeric pad.

4 - Open the On-Screen Keyboard.
Open the On-Screen Keyboard. The On-Screen Keyboard will open in a new window.

5 - Click the "Options" button in the On-Screen Keyboard.
Click the "Options" button in the On-Screen Keyboard. You'll find this below the PrtScn button.

6 - Check the "Turn on numeric keypad" box.
Check the "Turn on numeric keypad" box. When you click OK, the numeric keypad will appear on the right side of the On-Screen Keyboard.

7 - Click the keys in the on-screen numeric keypad to enter numbers.
Click the keys in the on-screen numeric keypad to enter numbers. When you click one of the buttons, that number or symbol will be inserted wherever your typing cursor is.
- You cannot use the On-Screen keyboard to type Alt codes, as it does not recognize more than on keypress at a time. If you need to enter special characters, open the Character Map by searching for "character map" in the Start menu.
