Change Startup Programs on Your Computer
https://www.wikihow.com/Change-Startup-Programs-on-Your-Computer

0 - On Windows

1 - Open Start .
Open Start . Click the Windows logo in the bottom-left corner of the screen.
- ;

2 - Type task manager into Start.
Type task manager into Start. This will search your computer for the Task Manager app.

3 - Click Task Manager.
Click Task Manager. It's the computer-shaped icon at the top of the Start window. Doing so will open Task Manager.
- You can also press Ctrl+â‡§ Shift+Del to open Task Manager directly.
- Task Manager may take a few seconds to finish loading.

4 - Click the Startup tab.
Click the Startup tab. This tab is at the top of the Task Manager window.

5 - Select a program.
Select a program. Click the name of a program that you want to disable.
- You can see a program's impact on your computer's startup speed to the right of the program's name (e.g., a high-impact program has "High" to the right of its name).

6 - Click Disable.
Click Disable. It's in the bottom-right corner of the window. Doing so will prevent the program from turning on the next time that you start your computer.
- If you want to turn back on a disabled program, you can select it and then click Enable in the bottom-right corner instead.
