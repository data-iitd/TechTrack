Stop Dock Apps from Bouncing on a Mac
https://www.wikihow.com/Stop-Dock-Apps-from-Bouncing-on-a-Mac

0 - Steps

1 - Click on the Spotlight icon.
Click on the Spotlight icon. This is the small magnifying glass located in the top-right corner of your Mac's screen.
- ;

2 - Type "terminal" into the search bar.
Type "terminal" into the search bar.

3 - Double-click "Terminal" in the results.
Double-click "Terminal" in the results. It should be located near or at the top of the results that appear.

4 - Type the code below into Terminal.
Type the code below into Terminal. Alternatively, you can copy and paste it into Terminal by clicking and dragging your mouse over the text below, hitting ⌘+C on your keyboard, then clicking in the Terminal window and hitting ⌘+V. The syntax to enter is as follows:
- defaults write com.apple.dock no-bouncing -bool TRUE

5 - Hit ⏎ Return on your keyboard.
Hit ⏎ Return on your keyboard. Doing so will run the command in Terminal, which tells your computer never to animate when they have notifications.
- To reverse this command, enter defaults write com.apple.dock no-bouncing -bool FALSE into Terminal at any time and hit ⏎ Return on your keyboard.
