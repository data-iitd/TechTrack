Set a Lock Screen Message on a Mac
https://www.wikihow.com/Set-a-Lock-Screen-Message-on-a-Mac

0 - Steps

1 - Click on the Apple menu.
Click on the Apple menu. It's the black, apple-shaped icon in the upper-left of your screen.
- ;

2 - Click on System Preferences.
Click on System Preferences.

3 - Click on Security & Privacy.
Click on Security & Privacy. It's near the top of the Preferences window.

4 - Click on General.
Click on General.

5 - Click on the padlock icon.
Click on the padlock icon. It's in the lower left of the dialog box.

6 - Enter admin credentials.
Enter admin credentials. Only an admin can make changes to security and privacy settings.

7 - Click on the checkbox next to "Show a message when the screen is locked."
Click on the checkbox next to "Show a message when the screen is locked." Make sure it's blue and displaying a checkmark.

8 - Click on Set Lock Message.
Click on Set Lock Message.

9 - Type your desired message.
Type your desired message. Then click on Ok.

10 - Click on the padlock icon.
Click on the padlock icon. You've now set a lock screen message for your Mac.
