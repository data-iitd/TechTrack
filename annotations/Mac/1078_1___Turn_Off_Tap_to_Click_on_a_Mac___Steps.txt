Turn Off Tap to Click on a Mac
https://www.wikihow.com/Turn-Off-Tap-to-Click-on-a-Mac

0 - Steps

1 - Click the Apple menu.
Click the Apple menu. This is in the upper-left corner of the screen, regardless of which program is open.
- ;

2 - Click the "System Preferences" option.
Click the "System Preferences" option. The System Preferences menu will appear.
- If the System Preferences menu doesn't open to the main screen, click the "Show All" button. This has an icon of 12 dots arranged in a grid.

3 - Click "Trackpad."
Click "Trackpad." This can be found in the second group of options.

4 - Click the "Tap to click" box.
Click the "Tap to click" box. When this is checked, tapping the trackpad will act like clicking a mouse button. Unchecking it will disable tap to click.

5 - Click the "Secondary click" box.
Click the "Secondary click" box. When this is checked, tapping two fingers will open the secondary click menu on an item.

6 - Click the "Look up" box.
Click the "Look up" box. When this is checked, tapping three fingers on an item will look up more details. For example, three-finger tapping a word will look up its definition.[1]
