0 - Downloading the Installation File

1 - Ensure that your Mac meets the software and hardware requirements.
Ensure that your Mac meets the software and hardware requirements. To do so, click the Apple menu , then click About This Mac. Your Mac must meet the following requirements:
- OS X 10.10 (Yosemite) and later
- One of the following Mac models:
- MacBook Pro (2012 and up)
- MacBook Air (2012 and up)
- MacBook (2015 and up)
- iMac (2012 and up)
- Mac mini (2012 and up)
- 
- ;

2 - Go to the Microsoft installer page.
Go to the Microsoft installer page. Enter https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/software-download/windows10 in your preferred browser. Make sure you perform this process on your Mac so that you receive the correct files when downloading. It will look like:

3 - Select an edition of Windows 10.
Select an edition of Windows 10. Scroll down, click the Select edition drop-down box, click a Windows 10 version, and then click Confirm.
- If you don't know which version to select, just click Windows 10.

4 - Select a language.
Select a language. Click the Choose one drop-down box, click your preferred language, and click Confirm.

5 - Click 64-bit Download.
Click 64-bit Download. It's in the middle of the page. Doing so will prompt the Windows 10 ISO file to download to your Mac; once it finishes, you can proceed with installing Windows 10.
