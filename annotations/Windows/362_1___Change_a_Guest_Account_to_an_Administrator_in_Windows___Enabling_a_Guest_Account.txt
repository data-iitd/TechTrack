0 - Enabling a Guest Account

1 - Sign into your computer with an Administrator account.
Sign into your computer with an Administrator account. The first account created on a computer is the Administrator by default.
- If you only have one user account, then it is the Administrator account.
- You cannot make changes to User Account Controls without administrator privileges.
- ;

2 - Press ⊞ Win+X and select “Control Panel” from the menu that appears.
Press ⊞ Win+X and select “Control Panel” from the menu that appears. The Control Panel contains various settings for your computer.
- Windows XP, Vista, and 7 users can press ⊞ Win and select “Control Panel” from the start menu.

3 - Click "User Accounts and Family Safety" from the available options.
Click "User Accounts and Family Safety" from the available options. This will take you to the User Accounts control panel page.[1]

4 - Click the “Remove user accounts” link.
Click the “Remove user accounts” link. This link appears under the “User Accounts” header.[3]

5 - Click “Guest Account”.
Click “Guest Account”. This will appear listed with the other accounts on your computer and will take you to a guest account screen prompting you if you want to enable the feature.

6 - Press “Turn On”.
Press “Turn On”. The Guest account can now be accessed from the login screen after logging out or restarting your computer.
