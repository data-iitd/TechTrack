0 - Resetting your PC

1 - Plug your computer into a power supply.
Plug your computer into a power supply. If you’re using a laptop, you’ll want to make sure you don’t run out of battery power during this process.
- ;

2 - Right-click the  button.
Right-click the  button. It’s usually at the bottom-left corner of the screen. Use this method to delete all of your data off of the PC and install a fresh version of Windows.

3 - Click Settings.
Click Settings. It’s toward the bottom of the menu.

4 - Click Security & updates.
Click Security & updates. It’s the icon with two curved arrows.

5 - Click Recovery.
Click Recovery. It’s in the left column.

6 - Click Get started under “Reset this PC.
Click Get started under “Reset this PC.” It’s the first button in the main panel.

7 - Click Restore factory settings.
Click Restore factory settings. This will erase the contents of the hard drive and then reinstall the factory version of Windows.

8 - Click Remove files and clean the drive.
Click Remove files and clean the drive. This formats the drive so that it will be more difficult for someone to recover your deleted data.

9 - Click Reset.
Click Reset. Your hard drive will now begin to format and then Windows will be reinstalled. This process may take several hours.
