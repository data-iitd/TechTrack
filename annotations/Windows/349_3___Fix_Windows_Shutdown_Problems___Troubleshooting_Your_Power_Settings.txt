Fix Windows Shutdown Problems
https://www.wikihow.com/Fix-Windows-Shutdown-Problems

0 - Troubleshooting Your Power Settings

1 - Open Start .
Open Start . Click the Windows logo in the bottom-left corner of the screen.

2 - Click Settings .
Click Settings . This icon is in the lower-left corner of the Start window.

3 - Click Update & security.
Click Update & security. It's an icon of a circular arrow.

4 - Click Troubleshoot.
Click Troubleshoot. This tab is on the left side of the window.

5 - Scroll down and click Power.
Scroll down and click Power. It's toward the bottom of the page.

6 - Click Run the troubleshooter.
Click Run the troubleshooter. This button will appear below and to the right of the Power option. Clicking it will start the troubleshooting process.

7 - Wait for an error to appear.
Wait for an error to appear. Common power issues include errors related to battery life and screen brightness.
- If no errors appear and the process completes, your power settings aren't responsible for your computer failing to shut down.

8 - Click Apply this fix.
Click Apply this fix. You'll do this for each problem that Windows comes across.
- If you see something listed as a problem that you don't care to fix, click Skip this fix instead.

9 - Try to shut down your computer.
Try to shut down your computer. If the computer successfully shuts down, the problem was resolved. If not, proceed to the next part.
